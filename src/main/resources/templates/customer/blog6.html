What To Make For Dinner If You Only Have 10 Minutes


We all have high hopes for what our regular weeknight dinners will look like, but when it comes to the actual execution, takeout, throwing together a sandwich, or opening a tub of yogurt often win out. Work runs late, you get home and you're tired, or you just have a bunch of things to do at the end of a long day, and the 30 minutes you were planning to devote to cooking yourself dinner dwindles down to 25, 20...oh, the subway was running late? Make that 10. The struggle is shared by all.

But committing an interesting list of 10 minute dinners to memory isn't something we all have the brain space for. Tack on the time it takes to look up and follow a recipe, and pouring a bowl of cereal sounds even more desirable. SO, I'm here to help you out—if you only have 10 minutes to spend in the kitchen, there's one dinner that's totally easy to make, tastes good, is as healthy (or as unhealthy) as you need it to be, and has an endless amount of variations, no recipe required: a stir fry!

Stir frys are brilliant for many reasons: You can make them with whatever you have in your refrigerator or pantry—which means no after-work grocery shopping and no buying ingredients for a special, complicated meal that you run out of time to make. They're also fast—sautéing veggies, chicken, shrimp, beans, rice, and even eggs takes under 10 minutes. It can be carb heavy (throw in some leftover pasta!) or low-carb (use veggies or cauli rice!).
